RV Rental Options

For 8–10 people: two 4–5 berth campervans is the recommended setup

Why 2 vehicles?
No single motorhome in Iceland legally seats and sleeps 10 people. The standard approach for groups of 8–10 is two mid-to-large campervans. This also makes parking, campsite navigation, and flexibility much easier on Ring Road — campervan parking is designed for standard-size vehicles, not coaches.

May vs. September — Which is better?

Both are excellent. The right choice depends on your group's priorities.

🌱 May (late May recommended)

✓17–22 hrs daylight → near midnight sun by May 20
✓Puffins arrive at Reynisfjara & Látrabjarg
✓Waterfalls at peak flow (snowmelt)
✓Slightly fewer tourists than September
✓Spring landscape — fresh green after snow
~Temperatures 5–12°C — cool but doable
✗Northern Lights virtually impossible (too bright)
✗Some highland F-roads still closed before June
✗Variable weather — possible late snow showers
✗Fewer campsite facilities open (check each site)

🍂 September (early Sep recommended)

✓Northern Lights possible from late August → prime viewing
✓All roads fully open — best F-road access
✓Golden autumn tundra — dramatic photography light
✓Whale watching peak (humpbacks, minkes, orcas)
✓12–15 hrs daylight — comfortable for driving
✓All campsites fully open with facilities
~Temperatures 6–13°C — similar to May
✗Puffins mostly departed by mid-August
✗Higher chance of autumn rain and wind
✗Slightly higher crowds vs May (still shoulder season)

Fuel & Road Practical Info
  • Fuel price: ~€1.90–2.30/liter (petrol & diesel)
  • Major chains: N1, Orkan, ÓB — 24/7 pay-at-pump with card
  • Get N1 card for small loyalty discount
  • East Fjords: stations can be 100+ km apart — always fill when above ¼ tank
  • Ring Road (Route 1) does NOT require 4WD in May or Sep
  • F-roads (Highlands) require 4WD high-clearance — illegal otherwise
  • Speed limit: 90 km/h on paved roads, 80 km/h on gravel roads
  • Drive on the right; ring roads are single lane each way
  • One-lane bridge rule: whoever is closer crosses first
Money, Safety & Weather
  • Currency: ISK (Icelandic Króna) · €1 ≈ ISK 145–150
  • Cards accepted virtually everywhere — no need for cash
  • Weather changes fast — always carry a waterproof layer
  • Reynisfjara black beach: sneaker waves are deadly — stay back from the surf line
  • Wild camping is heavily restricted — use official campsites only
  • Safetravel.is — register your itinerary before major hikes or F-roads
  • 112.is app — Iceland emergency app, works offline
  • Driving in high winds: pull over if your vehicle is being pushed sideways
  • Vedur.is — official Iceland weather forecast (check daily)
